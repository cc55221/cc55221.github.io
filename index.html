Che Chen (陈澈)

I am currently a Ph.D. student at Sun Yat-sen University (SYSU) and an exchange student at The University of Hong Kong (HKU), advised by Professor Shimin Gong and co-advised by Professor Kaibin Huang. In 2025, I was an exchange student at the University of Electro-Communications (UEC) in Tokyo, co-advised by Professor Zhi Liu.

I am working at the intersection of wireless communications, intelligent networking, and emerging multimodal systems. My research focuses on UAV-assisted wireless networks, 360° video streaming, and NOMA/RIS-enabled transmissions, with the broader goal of building adaptive, efficient, and intelligent communication systems for future wireless networks.

Research Interests

On the wireless networking side, I am interested in how aerial platforms, reconfigurable environments, and advanced multiple access techniques can jointly improve network coverage, spectrum efficiency, and user experience. In UAV-assisted wireless networks, I study how UAVs can serve as flexible communication nodes to support dynamic traffic demands, edge services, and multimedia delivery. For immersive applications such as 360° video streaming, I focus on resource allocation, viewport-aware transmission, and QoE optimization under limited wireless resources. I also explore NOMA- and RIS-enabled transmission schemes, where non-orthogonal access and programmable wireless environments provide new opportunities for interference management and performance enhancement.

On the platform side, I am interested in building and leveraging simulation environments that connect wireless networking algorithms with more realistic physical and system-level dynamics. I have explored AirSim for UAV mobility, perception, and environment-aware control, as well as NS-3 for network simulation, protocol evaluation, and wireless performance analysis. These platforms allow me to study communication and control problems in more integrated settings, where UAV trajectories, channel conditions, user demands, and network protocols can be jointly modeled and evaluated.

Methodologically, my work combines optimization, deep reinforcement learning, transformers, and LLMs. I use deep reinforcement learning to address sequential decision-making problems in wireless networks, such as trajectory planning, user association, power control, beamforming, and adaptive streaming. I am also interested in transformer-based models for capturing long-range dependencies in network states, user behavior, video viewing patterns, and temporal wireless dynamics. More recently, I have been exploring how large language models can be integrated into wireless systems, either as high-level reasoning agents for network management or as tools for enhancing algorithm design, environment understanding, and human-network interaction. I view this line of work as a step toward more autonomous wireless networks, where communication systems can perceive complex environments, reason over sequential decisions, and continuously improve through learning.
